Public Health Specialist

Erica Kufa

I am a public health expert with over 21 years of experience across Africa, focusing on monitoring and evaluation, strategic information, and public health systems strengthening. My work has contributed to HIV, TB, Malaria, and immunization programs globally.

Education

  • Postgraduate Diploma in Geographic Information Sciences, University of Leeds (2019)
  • MPH, University of the Western Cape, South Africa (2012)
  • International Course in Applied Epidemiology, Emory University, USA (2003)
  • Diploma in Marketing Management, IMM, South Africa (2002)
  • BSc in Mathematics & Computer Science, University of Zimbabwe (1996)

Experience

The Global Fund – Associate Specialist (2012–Present)
  • Led KPI reporting, managed the PIP database, and supported enterprise portfolio reviews.
  • Developed results dashboards, programmatic tools, and impact reports used across departments.
  • Collaborated with WHO, UNAIDS, UNICEF, and PEPFAR to harmonize HIV-related data reporting.
  • Supported grant reviews, country consultations, and strategy alignment for funding cycles.
WHO AFRO – Technical Officer, HIV/TB (2001–2011)
  • Established regional HIV surveillance and M&E systems across 46 African countries.
  • Produced regional epidemiological updates and trained national teams on data analysis.
  • Supported strategic investments and epidemic modeling with WHO and UNAIDS.
  • Coordinated the AFRO Regional HIV Surveillance Network and annual estimation workshops.
WHO Consultancies – (2011–2012)
  • June - October 2012 – WHO Somalia Liaison Office, Nairobi, Kenya
    • Developed and supported data quality assessment tools for regional surveillance focal points.
    • Mentored focal points in data quality assurance and managed outbreak alert and surveillance databases.
    • Compiled and analyzed weekly data on epidemic-prone diseases and produced epidemiological updates.
    • Participated in Health and Wash Cluster meetings, contributing to joint planning for the Somalia Population Survey.
  • February - March 2012 – WHO Botswana Country Office, Gaborone
    • Supported the Botswana Malaria Indicator Survey by installing GPS software and digital survey tools.
    • Trained field staff on PDAs for household mapping, data collection, backup, and troubleshooting.
  • August - December 2011 – WHO Horn of Africa Health Emergency Support, Nairobi, Kenya
    • Led surveillance data collection and weekly epidemiological updates from Horn of Africa countries.
    • Maintained a 4W Matrix mapping emergency interventions and strengthened country office capacity for emergency response.
WHO Southern Africa – EPI Data Manager (1999–2001)
  • Managed AFP, measles, and immunization surveillance data across Southern Africa.
  • Published monthly bulletins and trained country data managers.
  • Contributed to GAVI proposal support and WHO measles elimination studies.
